Fae Contracts
This is a page dedicated to explaining Fae Contracts! For most players, these are something to be wary of and careful about entering into. Such contracts can also be made by any players playing a Faerie race.

Be careful with these things. They can be quite tricky! ~ Nibble

Fae Contracts are exactly what they sound like, binding legal contracts written by a Fae and signed by a mortal.

There is a lot of thought that goes into Fae Contracts, and nobody (Fae or mortal) should enter into one flippantly. Below is a list of guidelines to assist you in the creation (or anticipation) of a contract.


Ground Rules
There are some basic rules that are true of all Fae Contracts. Let's cover those first:

  • Contracts are made under the observance and authority of the world of TWE itself, instead of by any legal group. This means that both parties involved are bound by the conditions of the contract as long as they are present in TWE and can expect immediate repercussions for breaching the terms enclosed therein. If one or both parties leave TWE for any length of time, unless it is provided for within the terms of the contract to be suspended, the contract is rendered void.
  • No contract can become binding unless both parties sign the contract with a physical implement under their own direct control while physically present, having read the full contents, being of uncompromised mind, of their own free will. A contract cannot be made that requires a party to sign another contract.
  • Fae contracts are always made between exactly two parties, the mortal and the fae. It is very rare for the Mortal Party to include more than 1 person, and the Fae Party can only be a single individual.
  • Sections are completed in order, but they can be revisited and revised at any point by any party prior to Finalization. No modifications can be made to any section after Finalization, with one single exception: The Mortal Party can add the True Name of the Fae Party to Section 1 after Finalization but before Enactment.



Section 1: Party Definition
This first section defines the mortal and fae parties. Information is given here to clearly set the identities of the individuals in each party. This information normally includes full name, lineage, nation of origin, or any other details to avoid ambiguity. It is rather short and simple in most cases, but can be worded strangely on occasion.

If any of the information in Section 1 does not match with those who sign the contract, then the contract has no power and is voided instantaneously.

The identification information of each party must be written into the contract by the OPPOSITE party.

The Fae Party must provide valid identification information, but is not required to disclose their True Name. It is in the best interest of the Mortal Party to learn and add the Fae Party's True Name for this section immediately following Finalization.


Section 2: Fae Incumbents
Section 2 covers all the conditions that the Fae Party are required to uphold.

This section typically manifests as a list of specifically worded and meticulously numbered Clauses. These Clauses each specify one condition that the Fae Party must adhere to or else be held in violation of the Contract.
