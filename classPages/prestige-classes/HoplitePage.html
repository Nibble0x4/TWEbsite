The Hoplite
In the eastern plains of Taria stands the legendary Tri-Villages, three villages that have spent generations raising master warriors specializing in different kinds of warfare.
From the nothernmost Tri-Village, Morta, come the Hoplites. These men and woman have dedicated their lives to studying and mastering the combat style set forth by their great founder, Mortran.
Mortran famously used for different stances in combat, heightening his skills to levels previously only attained through the use of magic. These four stances are now known as the four Disciplines and are the foundation of a hoplite's training.
Sponsored and guided by the goddess Artemis, the hoplites train to become masters of just a few of Mortran's legendary abilities.
Before they can be trained, hoplites must prove themselves. They often are trained as fighters, barbarians, rangers, cavaliers, or even rogues before coming to receive the title of hoplite.

Role: As a Prestige Class, Hoplites are always adventurers with some measure of experience prior to their training in Morta.
Because of this, they can have a variety of backgrounds. However, they are typically best if they are intelligent with a solid physical skillset and extensive combat training.
Fighters are the most common and natural fit, due to their natural mastery of martial weapon groups.
Magi are common as hoplites. They must train longer to meet the combat experience requirement, but their natural bent toward the academic lends itself to greater control as a hoplite.
Rangers may have gained skills by focusing on polearms with their combat styles, and they are not uncommon as hoplites.
Rogues, like magi, must train longer but often find themselves fitting in well with their cleverness and affinity for the Moon stance.
Cavaliers often favor longer polearms naturally, and may be drawn to hoplite to complement their leadership and martial prowess, especially with the Huntress stance.
Finally, the door to hoplite training swings open to anyone willing to put in the effort. It is uncommon for other classes, perhaps, but not unheard of!

Class Features



Weapon and Armor Proficiency
A Hoplite gains proficiency with all polearm weapons that have reach, but does not gain any armor or shield proficiencies.


Polearm Dedication
Hoplites are exclusive in their use of polearm weapons. None of their hoplite abilities can be used unless he is wielding a polearm weapon with the Reach trait.
However, they are also capable of handling any polearm. They gain proficiency with any polearm weapon they find, though they can still take penalties if they fail to meet the requirements for wielding it.


Disciplines
The training of the hoplite is rigorous, but even amongst their ranks there are different traditions. Different practitioners emphasize different skills in combat.
Masters of different skills have formed Disciplines, cauterizing the arts that allow their masters to perform martial feats unique to them.

At 1st level, a hoplite chooses one of the Disciplines and gains rank 1 in it.
At each level afterward, he chooses one of the Disciplines and increases his rank in that Discipline by 1, or gains rank 1 in a Discipline he does not already have.

Each Stance takes 1 action to enter and grants the following:
Access to abilities and bonuses from the corresponding discipline
A Bonus and Limitation that scales with level




    Huntress Discipline

    1. Huntress Stance
      Grants the ability to enter Huntress Stance as an action. Leaving Huntress Stance is one action as well.
      While in Huntress Stance, you may add twice your Huntress Rank to your athletics checks made to leap or climb, and to ride or fly checks made to remain mounted or to use the Mounted Combat feat.
      While in Huntress Stance, if you add any impact damage to an attack, add 1d6 extra impact damage. In addition, if you charge, deal 1d6 impact damage.
      While in Huntress Stance, you may not add your strength or dexterity modifier to damage.
      While in Huntress Stance, remove any physical DR you may have from non-magical sources.
    2. Vault (1)
      As a Swift Action while in Huntress Stance, you may pole vault off a surface up to 20 feet into the air, landing in a space within 20 feet. This movement does not provoke an attack of opportunity.
      If you wish to leap higher, provoke attacks of opportunity to roll an athletics check. For each 5 of the result, increase the distance by 5 feet.
      If the space is occupied by a creature at least 1 size larger than you, you may attempt an Athletics check against their CMD to mount them.
      If the space is occupied by a creature of your size or smaller, you may make an attack roll against the creature. If you strike, deal damage and land in any adjacent space. If there is no unoccupied space, fall prone. If you do not strike, fall prone.
    3. Clinging Pest
      In Huntress Stance, while mounted on a creature that does not want to be mounted, you may choose to apply a penalty of twice your Huntress Discipline to one roll made by the creature during their turn as an immediate action.
    4. Biting Leach
      In Huntress Stance, while mounted on a creature that does not want to be mounted, you may absorb the physical damage dealt by the first strike you land on that creature each turn as healing.
    5. Drop Strike (1)
      When falling while in Huntress Stance, you may negate all fall damage taken and add the damage to an attack made at a creature you land on.
      Alternatively, you may spin your weapon over your head like a helicopter and land softly, taking no fall damage.
      Huntress Mastery
      You have mastered Huntress Stance. Your bonuses from Huntress Stance that increase based on your Huntress Rank now use your total Hoplite level instead.
      When you gain this level, choose 1 ability from the following list to be available to you while not in Huntress Stance:
      • Vault
      • Clinging Pest
      • Biting Leach

      Legendary Maneuver
      Huntress' Stun (Once Per Day)
      When dealing damage with a melee attack that deals impact damage or on a charge, stun the target for 1d3+1 rounds.
      (Fortitude DC = 20 + Int modifier). If the attack deals impact damage, increase the DC by 1 for every 10 impact damage dealt by the attack.



    Bulwark Discipline

    1. Bulwark Stance
      Grants the ability to enter Bulwark Stance as an action. Leaving Bulwark Stance is one action as well.
      While in Bulwark Stance, add your Bulwark Rank to your saves and CMD against effects that attempt to move you or knock you over.
      While in Bulwark Stance, gain DR/- equal to twice your Bulwark Rank.
      While in Bulwark Stance, your movement speed is reduced to 5 feet. If you are mounted, this has no impact on the movement speed of the mount.
    2. Counterstrike (1)
      As an Immediate Action while in Bulwark Stance, you may spend an Attack of Opportunity to make an attack roll against a creature in range who dealt damage to you with an attack. Add twice your Bulwark Rank and any damage reduced from the attack by your DR as extra base damage for this attack.
      If your attack lands, you may push the target 5 feet back.
    3. Canny Bastion
      When in Bulwark Stance, add your Intelligence modifier to your AC.
    4. Thorny Bastion
      When Clashing during Bulwark Stance, add your Bulwark Rank to your clash roll.
      If your clash roll defeats your opponent's attack roll by 5 or more, compare the result to the attacker's AC and deal damage if it is greater.
    5. Unbreakable (2)
      As an immediate action while in Bulwark Stance, when you take damage from an attack that you see coming, you may choose to cut the damage in half.
      Bulwark Mastery
      You have mastered Bulwark Stance. Your bonuses from Bulwark Stance that increase based on your Bulwark Rank now use your total Hoplite level instead.
      When you gain this level, choose 1 ability from the following list to be available to you while not in Bulwark Stance:
      • Counterstrike
      • Canny Bastion
      • Thorny Bastion

      Legendary Maneuver
      Bulwark's Deflection (Once Per Day)
      When you are subject to a damaging ranged attack or area attack that originates from a point and travels toward you, you can choose to deflect it.
      If there is a save, make a Fortitude Save against the DC as if it was trying to move you.
      If there is an attack roll, make a clash roll against the attack.
      If you succeed, you may reflect the attack or effect at any target within 90 degrees and are not considered to be harmed by it.
      If you critically succeed, you may reflect within 180 degrees.



    Warden Discipline

    1. Warden Stance
      Grants the ability to enter Warden Stance as an action. Leaving Warden Stance is one action as well.
      While in Warden Stance, you gain an additional number of Attacks of Opportunity per turn equal to your Warden Rank.
      While in Warden Stance, increase the reach of your weapon by 5 feet.
      While in Warden Stance, you suffer a -4 penalty to your AC.
    2. Intercede (2)
      As an Immediate Action while in Warden Stance, you may spend an attack of opportunity to make a Clashing roll against an enemy melee attack roll made against an ally in your reach.
    3. Opportunistic Warden
      When making an attack of opportunity in Warden Stance, you may take an Attack action instead of being limited by the normal restrictions of an attack of opportunity.
    4. Greedy Warden
      Enemies can provoke attacks of opportunity from you for the same reason one additional time per turn.
      (Ex. An enemy running through spaces you threaten can provoke attacks for that movement twice in one move instead of once.)
    5. Great Sweep (2)
      As an action while in Warden Stance, increase reach by 5 feet and make a single attack roll. Compare the result to the AC of every creature within reach.
      Allies warned before the attack can avoid it.
      Warden Mastery
      You have mastered Warden Stance. Your bonuses from Warden Stance that increase based on your Warden Rank now use your total Hoplite level instead.
      When you gain this level, choose 1 ability from the following list to be available to you while not in Warden Stance:
      • Intercede
      • Opportunistic Warden
      • Greedy Warden

      Legendary Maneuver
      Warden's Lockdown (Once Per Day)
      Choose one of the following combat maneuvers and perform it against all enemies within reach:
      • Trip
      • Bullrush
      • Disarm

      Each target against whom the maneuver succeeds has their movement speed halved and suffers -1 action on their next turn.



    Moon Discipline

    1. Moon Stance
      Grants the ability to enter Moon Stance as an action. Leaving Moon Stance is one action as well.
      While in Moon Stance, add a bonus to stealth checks and sleight of hand checks equal to twice your Moon Rank.
      While in Moon Stance, double the base damage dice of your weapon.
      While in Moon Stance, you can make only one attack during your turn.
    2. Fade (1)
      As an action during Moon Stance, when in a large shadow or area of low-light or darker, you may roll stealth to attempt to fade from view until your next strike or the end of your turn, whichever comes first.
      If the area is dark, gain a +2 bonus on this check.
    3. Full Moon
      When striking an unaware foe during Moon Stance, add a number of d8 to your damage equal to your Moon Rank.
    4. Crescent Moon
      When striking an unaware foe during Moon Stance, you may take a move action. If you move behind cover, you may roll stealth to remain undetected.
    5. Eclipse (2)
      As an action while in Moon Stance, pick an ally and an enemy you can see, causing them to glow brightly with light that darkens their surroundings.
      The area within 15 feet of these creatures becomes supernatural darkness, and the creatures are unable to make stealth checks.
      Moon Mastery
      You have mastered Moon Stance. Your bonuses from Moon Stance that increase based on your Moon Rank now use your total Hoplite level instead.
      When you gain this level, choose 1 ability from the following list to be available to you while not in Moon Stance:
      • Fade
      • Full Moon
      • Crescent Moon

      Legendary Maneuver
      Moon's Dark Side (Once Per Day)
      As an action, your shadow manifests into a quasi-real shadow construct with statistics identical to yours. For 2 turns, as long as you are in Moon Stance, this construct takes any attack action you take.



Control
The Hoplites are famed for their control over their long, unwieldy weapons. There are some maneuvers they can perform that are more difficult. These taxing actions cost Control.
At 2nd level, a hoplite gains a pool of Control equal to his Intelligence modifier plus his Hoplite level. He can use points from this pool to use hoplite abilities that have a cost.


Control
The Hoplites are famed for their control over their long, unwieldy weapons. There are some maneuvers they can perform that are more difficult. These taxing actions cost Control.
At 2nd level, a hoplite gains a pool of Control equal to his Intelligence modifier plus his Hoplite level. He can use points from this pool to use hoplite abilities that have a cost.

  1. Controlled Strike (1) - As a swift action, a hoplite can spend 1 point from his Control pool to add his Intelligence modifier to an attack roll.
  2. Piercing Strike (1) - As a swift action, a hoplite can spend 1 point from his Control pool to bypass the DR of an enemy with an attack.
  3. Controlled Maneuver (1) - As a swift action, a hoplite can spend 1 point from his Control pool to add his Intelligence modifier to a CMB roll.
  4. Controlled Defense (1) - As an immediate action, a hoplite can spent 1 point from his Control pool to add his Intelligence modifier to his AC against a single attack.



Moment of Respite
At 4th level, hoplites learn to take advantage of the peace that comes after a hard-fought victory to replenish their minds.
After a battle is complete, the hoplite can restore half of the Control Points he spent in the battle (Rounding down).


Legendary Maneuver
When a hoplite becomes level 10, their skills ascend into legend. For every style they have attained mastery of, they gain access to the Legendary Maneuver for that style.
If a hoplite has mastered multiple styles, they gain access to multiple Legendary Maneuvers. However, using a Legendary Maneuver causes such strain to the body that he cannot use another in the same turn.
In order to use a Legendary Maneuver, the hoplite must be in the corresponding stance.

OR

Alternatively, a hoplite can choose to eschew Legendary Maneuvers and gain 2 additional ranks to put into Disciplines of his choice.

At first level, the Hoplite selects two runes from the Rune Archives as ones that she knows. She can learn new runes by finding them in the world of TWE or by levelling up as a Hoplite.
At every new Hoplite level, she learns 1 new rune.

Tracing a rune normally takes 2 actions and requires the Hoplite to use some sort of drawing implement.
In order to trace a rune, a medium must be selected. There are 5 total mediums, though not every rune is suited to every medium.

  1. Air - This medium requires nothing more than the finger of the tracer and is very quick. Tracing a rune this way takes only 1 action and may not cost any RE. Runes traced in this way dissipate immediately after being used. If their effects last more than one turn, then the rune appears on the surface of whatever was created as a result of the rune.
  2. Surface - Any solid surface capable of bearing writing (the ground, sand, glass, a wall, etc) can bear a rune. Runes traced on a surface are not singular runes only. Instead, 2d6 are rolled when the Hoplite begins to trace a Surface rune. They spend the RE cost when they trace the first rune, but can then inscribe a number of surface runes up to the rolled result without spending any additional RE. All runes must be within 20 feet of at least one other rune. The area included within these runes is used to create the effect the moment all the runes are complete.
  3. Weapon - Runes can be traced on a weapon, infusing it with power. Most weapons cannot handle more than one rune on them without becoming unstable.
  4. Apparel - Runes can be traced on clothing, armor, and accessories. Anything worn could potentially receive a rune if it is large enough.
  5. Item - Any item that is not included in the above categories counts as an Item and can be infused with a rune as well.


A rune inscribed on a surface, weapon, apparel, or item can be destroyed by dealing physical damage to it. A rune has a number of HP equal to twice the creator's Hoplite level.
In addition, a rune can be disenchanted by a dispel magic spell or by being magically erased. Doing so requires a caster level check opposed to a DC of 10 + Twice the Hoplite's Hoplite level + the Hoplite's Int + the RE invested in the rune.
If a rune calls for a save, the DC is equal to 10 + the RE stored in the rune + the Hoplite's Int modifier.
Some runes have features that may modify this, granting extra ways to be disenchanted or making them more resilient in some way.






Runic Energy
The Hoplite gains a pool of energy equal to twice her Hoplite level plus her intelligence modifier (min 1).
This energy is spent in order to empower and sustain Runes. These points are allotted to a rune when the rune is traced and can be recalled as a swift action, leaving the rune inert.
Runic Energy (RE) remains in a rune until it is recalled or the rune is destroyed or disenchanted by another. If the tracer is knocked unconscious or even killed, the RE remains in the rune until one of these things occur.
For the most part, any points spent from this pool are returned upon the destruction or disenchantment of one of the Hoplite's runes. However, there are some effects that call for the Hoplite to 'Burn' her RE.
Each day, when she is preparing her spells, the Hoplite can regain a number of burnt RE equal to her Hoplite level.

Spellruning
At 3rd level, the Hoplite learns to use her runes to enhance her other spellcasting.
As an immediate action when the Hoplite casts a spell, she may trace a singular rune as part of the somatic components of the spell. If the spell does not have somatic components, one is added.
Doing this is called 'runing a spell.' Runing a spell can only be done with a rune that matches the spell type or subtype in some way (Example: An Ash rune could be used to rune a 'Burning Hands' spell, but not a 'Feather Fall' spell.)
Runing a spell requires the Hoplite to Burn 1 RE from her pool.

When a spell has been successfully Runed, choose one of the following bonuses:

  1. Increase the Caster Level of the spell by 1.
  2. Increase the DC of the spell by 1.
  3. Increase the number of possible targets by 1.



Runic Research
At 5th level, the Hoplite has become so skilled at tracing that she is ready to try her hand at discovery. During downtime, she can roll Spellcraft to make progress on researching a new rune.
Once her research has attained a total of 200, she may work with the DM to discover a brand new rune not yet included in the Rune Archive. She can choose whether to share her research, adding the new rune to the Archive, or not. If her research is stolen, she retains knowledge of the rune but it will not be exclusive to her anymore.

For every successful rune she discovers, the total she must attain to discover a new rune increases by 100.


Spelltracing
At 7th level, the Hoplite has become adept at capturing the essence of magic in runic form. Whenever she observes a spell being cast, she may make a spellcraft check with a DC equal to 10 + the caster level of the caster + the level of the spell.
If she succeeds, she has an idea of how to trace the spell. She can then choose to spend 1 full-round action and burn RE equal to twice the level of the spell in order to trace a rune containing the essence of the spell upon a surface. If she does not do this within 1d4 rounds, she loses her idea.

The rune containing the essence of the spell is volatile, and will react unpredictably if destroyed. The rune can be activated as an action, releasing the spell. If the rune still contains the spell, it can be studied and the spell within learned as if it were written in a wizard's spellbook.
