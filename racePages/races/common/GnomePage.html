The Gnome Race


History
Gnomes have been around longer than history cares to record. Even Precedent writings can be found referencing beings that have been theorized to be Gnomish ancestors. It is unsure exactly when or how these little creatures began, but they are here to stay. Gnomes live longer than most races, rarely dying before the age of 100. They thrive in more remote regions of TWE, but they do not struggle with incorporating themselves into human society. Gnomes are a common sight in human settlements all across TWE, though there are certainly some gnomish communities that choose to live the seclusionist lifestyle in an attempt to keep their culture pure.

Appearance
Gnome appearance relies heavily on ancestry, though all of them are small. Fae-Touched gnomes, the ones most people are familiar with, sport skin and hair colors seemingly unbound by any limitations whatsoever. Blue-skinned gnomes with bright pink hair fail to even raise an eyebrow. Earthen gnomes are more subdued. Their skin and hair can still still vary, but the color tends to be darker, less vibrant, and wilder colors like pink or blue are less common. The Betharrelle possess gray skin and eyes that sit somewhere on the spectrum between black and a deep red. Their hair, while it can still be vibrant, is stringy and thin as a reminder of what they have lost. Gnomish fashion defies any and all tradition, as if each and every gnome were determined to be the first to sport their particular style.

Culture
Gnomish culture is difficult to pin down. Considering how individualistic they tend to be, it can be hard to put them all into the same box. Many gnomes choose to ignore culture entirely, just observing and slightly defying whatever norms exist within another culture. Others, tired of the constant need to be unique, settle into a life of comfortable conformity. The only real through-line for Gnomish culture manifests in a widespread respect for the natural world, a love of laughter, and a profound sense of simultaneous awe and irreverence directed at anything larger than they are.

Functionality
Gnomes are a strange lot. They are all small and magically inclined, but that magic can manifest in very different manners. Some possess a connection to the fae world, others shun the sun, and still others seem to have a nearly instinctual understanding of complex systems. Gnomes are as varied in ability as they are in appearance.

Racial Traits
These are the basic traits natural to Gnomes

Base Speed
Gnomes have a base speed of 20 feet on land.

Size
Gnomes are Small creatures and thus gain a +1 size bonus to attack rolls and ac, a +4 size bonus to stealth, but suffer a -1 size penalty on CMB and CMD and a -4 size penalty on intimidate checks against larger creatures.

Type
Gnomes are Humanoids with the Gnome subtype.

Defense Racial Traits
Size Training: Gnomes are experienced fighting giants, and thus gain a +2 racial bonus to their AC against foes who are at least 2 size categories larger than them.

Various Racial Traits

Low-Light Vision: Gnomes can see twice as far as humans in areas of low light.

Languages: Gnomes begin play speaking Common, Gnome, and Sylvan. Gnomes with high Intelligence scores can choose from the following: Draconic, Dwarven, Elven, Giant, Goblin, and Orc.

Hatred: Gnomes gain a +2 bonus on attack and damage rolls against Goblins, their hated foes.

Gnome Magic: Choose 1 orison from the druid spell list. You may cast this using your highest mental stat. Alternatively, you may choose to cast Speak with Animals once per day.



Ancestry
Gnomes come from one of several lines, more or less traceable through history. These gnomes have wildly different origins, shaping their bodies and inherent magic in different ways. When a gnome is born, their ancestry matches that of one of their parents. There seem to be no gnomes that manage to bridge the divide between the ancestroes, always being claimed by one line or another in full.







Fae-Touched Gnome
These Gnomes have been imprinted upon by the capricious and deadly world of the fae. They are not strong, but they can tap into otherworldly power and their very blood sings for joy at the thought of a good prank.

Ability Scores: They are quick and charming, but are weak due to their small bodies. They gain +2 Dex, +2 Cha, -2 Str.

Capricious Tendencies: Gain a +2 bonus on saving throws and DCs for and against Fae effects and Illusion effects.

Fae Weakness: Any DR you possess is bypassed by Cold Iron.

Just a Bit of Fun: You begin play knowing 1 Fae Orison from the following list:

  • - Faerie Ring
  • - Look at This
  • - Name Calling
  • - Faelights
  • - Tricksy Form
  • - Displae



Earthen Gnome
Earthen Gnomes have a strong affiliation for the earth and its metals. Often relating more to dwarves than their fae-touched kin, Earthen Gnomes are fascinated by systems and often spend long hours obsessing over the work of their hands. Making things shift and move in just the right way brings a grin to their faces and satisfaction to their hearts.

Ability Scores: They are resilient and studied, but are slow to react to changes in their surroundings. They gain +2 Con, +2 Int, -2 Dex.

Crafty: Gain a +4 bonus on Craft checks of a single type.

Tinkertoy: By spending an hour with basic materials, you may construct a Tiny tinkertoy that fulfills one function. It is incredibly fragile and performs its task for only an hour before breaking down and requiring a few seconds of maintenance.

False Familiar: If you have a material harvested from a creature and fitting materials, you may spend an hour to create a Tiny False Familiar in their likeness. This is a mechanical familiar imbued with enough aether to function as an actual familiar. You may sustain only one False Familiar at a time.

Weapon Familiarity: Earthen Gnomes treat any weapon with the word 'Gnome' in the name as a simple weapon.



Betharrelle
Long ago, a fae-touched gnome named Bethara was cursed by a being of far, far greater power. All of her offspring share in her curse, forever banished from the warm light of the sun. The Betharrelle hide beneath the surface world, their bodies far stronger than those of their kin, but ever envious of the life of ease afforded by those who look at the sky without fear.

Ability Scores: The Betharrelle channel their magic inward, enhancing their bodies. They gain +2 Str, +2 Dex, +2 Con.

Tough: Gain +1 max HP per level. In addition, gain a +2 bonus on Fortitude saves.

Cave Runner: Your base movement speed is 35 feet.

Climbing Claws: Your sharp fingers act like claws that grant you a 20 ft climb speed. In addition, when climbing a creature, you may choose to deal damage to it equal to your Str or Dex modifier.

Darkvision: 60 feet.

Light Blindness: When in an area of Bright Light or natural sunlight, you cannot see more than 5 feet away.

Light Weakness: When targeted by a spell or effect with the Light descriptor, suffer a -4 penalty on saves against it.

Undersoul: You are healed by negative energy damage and harmed by positive energy damage, similar to undead.

Solar Petrification: You have a pool of points called 'Petro Points' that begins empty and is filled when you are subjected to certain effects. Each night, when the sun goes down, lose all Petro Points. Casting Flesh to Stone or Stone to Flesh grants or removes a number of Petro Points equal to the level of the spell. Here are some ways to acquire Petro Points:

  • - For each round you end in direct sunlight, gain 1 Petro Point.
  • - If you are affected by a spell or effect with the Light descriptor, gain a number of Petro Points equal to the level of the spell or effect. If the spell deals light-based damage, instead gain Petro Points equal to half the damage taken.
  • - Anytime you would suffer damage from positive energy, take half of the damage as Petro Points.


As you accrue Petro Points, suffer various effects at different milestones:

  • <2> Sickened 2
  • <10> Fatigued
  • <15> Gain 1 Negative Level (DC to remove: Fort 15)
  • <25> Slowed
  • <40> Petrified (Not Dead)



Nisse
Home is a magical place, so it isn't surprising that some gnomes developed the ability to tap into that magic. Nisse, or 'household gnomes' are attuned to the magic of house and home in a way that makes them almost indispensable to established households.

Ability Scores: The Nisse are quick and pleasant, but aren't accustomed to physical contention. They gain +2 Dex, +2 Cha, -2 Con.

As a Mouse: When moving through a household, gain a +4 insight bonus to Stealth and a +2 bonus to Reflex saves against traps and magical effects inherent to the house.

Home Defender: When in combat in a household, gain a +2 dodge bonus to AC and reflex saves against spells. If your opponent is 'unwelcome' in the home, double these bonuses.

Hideaways: You are an expert at noticing the little spaces just out of sight, the places people normally can't quite reach. You gain a +4 bonus on perception checks to notice hidden passageways or crevices.
In addition, your little body is supernaturally flexible. You can squeeze through openings that are 6 inches wide or greater, moving up to half your speed.

Home Gardean: If the Nisse is granted the position of 'The Steward' over a household or garden, they gain a set of special powers while within that household:

  • As a move action, if you can move or squeeze into a space where you cannot be directly observed, you can disappear and reappear at any other unobserved location in the household that you are familiar with and have been to.
  • <10> Fatigued
  • When moving in this way, you can choose to pick up any single item that is currently hidden on the premises that they are aware of. Alternatively, you can choose to drop a single item they are holding, allowing it to be placed in a hidden spot you are aware of.
  • You can choose to spend an hour caring for the space, cleaning and weeding and whatnot. If you do so, you infuse the space with magic that acts as a gentle ward. Gardens grow healthier and more fruitful, and homes degrade more slowly. In addition, the area gains a ward that grants all 'welcome' creatures in the area a +1 bonus on d20 rolls and +1 HP per level regained during a long rest. For each day that you continue to tend to the place, the Ward's bonuses increase by 1 (Up to a maximum of 1/2 your level).


A household or garden can have only a single Steward. If you are replaced or fired as Steward, the ward you have made dissipates and the growth you have brought to the garden largely deteriorates.
For every day that you do not spend an hour tending to the household or garden, reduce the bonus of the Ward by 1. If this bonus reaches 0, the effects are similar to being replaced or removed as Steward

Squatter's Rights: If you are in a household that has no Steward, you may perform a 1-hour ritual to try to take the position.
Roll a d20 + Cha + level. The DC depends on the size and nature of the home, but the normal DC is 15.
If the owner is in the home and is unaware of you, increase the DC by 5. If the owner is aware of you, the check fails automatically.
If you are discovered during your ritual, the ritual fails.

Friend of the Community: Gain a bonus on Diplomacy checks made in the community where you are a Steward equal to 1 + your Ward bonus.
In any communities adjacent to that one, gain half the bonus (rounded down).
