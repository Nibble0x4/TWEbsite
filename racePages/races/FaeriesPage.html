Faeries in That Which Exists
The presence of the Fae in TWE can be felt in different intensities in different regions of the world. However, basic knowledge of 'Faeries' is enough for most sensible people to shut their mouth and raise their guard.

Fae races, collectively referred to as Faeries, are otherworldly beings with a frame of reference that is simply different from that of other races. The magic of another world is inextricably woven through their being, granting them strange powers and unintuitive weaknesses. They play the game of life by a different set of rules entirely, and often are known for not taking the worries and fears of other races seriously.

Faeries come in wildly different types, different enough to be their own unique races, but every Faerie shares a set of common traits that seem normal to them and strange to the other residents of TWE.

Below are two sections. One displays a list of known Faerie races, and the other gives an explanation of some traits common and unique to Faeries.

Faerie Compendium
These are the playable Faeries in TWE.

Pixies
The traditional image when one mentions 'Fairy.' Tiny winged humanoids with potent magical power.

Sprites
Tiny spirits of the world that inhabit natural environmental features.

Haglings
The daughters of monstrous women known as 'Hags.'

Changelings
Creatures with the ability to alter their form instinctively.

Faerie Mechanics
This is a list of the traits common to ALL Faerie races.

Type
Faeries all have the Faerie subtype.

True Name
Faeries have a secret 'True' name. This name cannot be changed and, if spoken, grants some level of power over the faerie.
This True Name can be strange, but must be one word and reasonably sized. When choosing or claiming any name, they must reference their True Name. They cannot accept being called anything that is not a reference to their Secret Name. If they are referred to in this way and are aware of it, then they must actively reject the name. If a creature begins fishing for their True Name by rapidly naming them, they may choose to ignore the naming.
The True Name is referenced by other abilities.

'Naming'
Speaking a faerie's True Name is referred to as 'Naming' them. Naming a faerie inflicts the following on them:

  • They suffer a -6 penalty on saves against your spells or effects.
  • They must succeed at a Will Save (DC 10+your level) to resist any of the following commands from you:
  • Faeries and Non-Faeries alike can also be Named using a normal name they give, but doing so gives none of the above effects.
    • Stay - They may not move from their space. If they are attacked by an enemy, they are freed.
    • Begone - They must do everything in their power to stay at least 60 feet away from you and avoid seeing or being seen by you.
    • Peace - They must drop out of initiative and may not take violent actions while in your presence or towards you or allies.
    • Speak - They must answer your questions. If they are caught in a lie (by contradiction, proof, or sense motive) then they take 2 nonlethal damage per level that cannot be healed until a full rest.
    • Silence - They may not make any noise or cast any spells.
    • Show Yourself - They are forcibly returned to their true form and any illusions, invisibility, or disguises are removed.
  • Faeries and Non-Faeries alike can also be Named using a normal name they give, but doing so gives none of the above effects. Being named in this way does count for spells or effects that require Naming.



Outsider
Faeries are considered extraplanar creatures that may be bound by some rituals.

Timeless
Faeries do not age in the same manner as mortals. They reach maturity before coming to TWE, and do not age after that. Instead, their connection to the Other from which they come slowly weakens over 100 years. Their power begins to fade as that time approaches, until they have no choice but to vanish from TWE until their power and capricious interest in this world return.

Contract
Faeries are capable of writing up and entering into contracts with mortal creatures.

Cold Iron Weakness
Faeries are weak to the special metal and enchantments known as "Cold Iron."

  • - They cannot be profient with any items made of Cold Iron.
  • - When they are struck by Cold Iron, any spells or effects they have active on themselves are dispelled.
  • - They suffer a -2 penalty on attack rolls when wielding weapons made of mundane metal.
  • - Their movement speed is reduced by 10 feet when wearing any armor made of mundane metal. If the armor is Cold Iron, then they can take no actions with the portions of the body that are wearing the armor.



Fae Orisons
Faeries are intensely magical, simply by their nature. Most Fae races will have some of the below Fae Orisons available to them at level 1. Unless otherwise stated, casting these Orisons takes only 1 Action, but they are unable to spend more than 1 of their 3 Actions on casting Fae Orisons on any given turn.

  • - Tricksy Form - Transform into a shape that lowers your potency, but allows for tricky mixups.
  • - Ouchie Form - Transform into a form that heightens some capabilities and lowers others.
  • - Familiar Form - Transform into a tiny creature capable of bonding as a familiar.
  • - Look at This - Draw attention to someone or something, making it hard to look at anything else.
  • - Confusionate - Confuse a mortal for a short time.
  • - Name Calling - Wrack the body of a mortal by twisting their name. More potent against Fae.
  • - Playtime - Begin a potentially deadly but fair game with a loose-tongued mortal.
  • - Faelights - Play tricks on mortals using deceitful lights.
  • - Faerie Ring - Create rings of shrooms that allow transportation between them.
  • - Pixie Dust - Scatter a dust over small objects to manipulate them.
  • - Will O' Wisp - Use a gleaming ball of light to lead mortals astray.
  • - Displae - Evoke strong emotional reactions through your performance.
