The Sprite Race


History
Though sprites are fae, hailing from the Other, they have been here in TWE far longer than other fae races. They came and ingratiated themselves with the land when it was younger, more vital and fresh. In the present, it is often forgotten that they are truly fae at all by the layman. They are less antagonistic of mortals than other fae, and as a result have managed to slip into many mortal cultures in unexpected ways.
Mortals are the primary source of new habitations for sprites, creating totems and shrines and landmarks that house the beings as a sort of ritual for good luck or companionship. This does not, of course, keep sprites from engaging in their fair share of mischief, but they have gained a greater understanding and appreciation for the beauty and brevity of mortal life than most others from the Other.

Culture
In TWE, sprites are often considered to be spirits of the very land itself. A sprite who steps out of a river is hailed as a Naiad, a sprite from a tree is called a Dryad, and most mortals don't even realize that these sprites could be one and the same. Sprites feel very little need for community, even amongst their own kind, and often choose to inhabit habitations in relatively remote regions of TWE. Of course, they can't be too far away if they want to enjoy fresh habitations, but that is a line each sprite must walk for themselves.

Functionality
Sprites are highly adaptable to and dependent on their surroundings. They gain different physicality depending on the most recent habitation they entered, meaning they can be tougher, faster, or stronger in different regions of TWE. They can merge into and out of their surroundings, making it very easy for them to escape or entirely avoid foes they don't wish to engage with. They can do well as virtually any class or role, save for some roles such as wizard or magus that require a formal education most of them simply don't receive.

Racial Traits
These are the basic traits natural to sprites

Ability Scores
Sprites are attuned to the natural world, but rarely educated outside their small spheres.

+2 Wis and -2 Int

Base Speed
sprites have a base movement speed of 15 feet.

They also have wings, giving them a 30 feet fly speed with perfect maneuverability. They struggle to fly at high elevations and must make Fly checks to avoid being tossed about by high winds. If they are wearing armor that reduces their movement speed, it applies twice to their fly speed.

Size
Sprites can be Small or Medium creatures. If they choose to be small, this grants them a +1 size bonus to AC and attack rolls. It also gives them a -1 size penalty on CMB and CMD. They also gain a +4 size bonus on stealth checks and a -4 penalty on intimidate checks against larger creatures. If they are Medium, they gain no bonuses or penalties due to their size.

Spiritual Understanding
Sprites have spent a great deal of time around Spirits, and have come to know them well.

When you gain a Spirit, you may increase one of their core stats by 1.

'Druidic' Magic
Sprites know a few innate spells that can be cast as Orisons, spells that mortals later witnessed and imitated in the form of better-known druidic orisons.

  • Chameleon Skin
  • Stable Ground
  • Sprite's Blessing



Habitations

Inhabit: As 2 actions, you can merge into a Habitation. While in a Habitation, you cannot cast spells or make attacks because your body has dissolved into the Habitation. You cannot be seen by normal means while in a Habitation unless you make yourself known. You have no need to breathe, eat, or drink while Inhabiting. Instead, your bodily health is linked to that of the Habitation. Exiting a Habitation is also 2 actions. As a full-round action, you may meditate and observe through your most recent Habitation regardless of distance. If your most recent Habitation is destroyed, you feel the pain of it and suffer 2 points of damage per character level. Habitations come in three different kinds:

  • Stone - Stone Habitations are totems, memorials, or lone stone structures such as dilapidated arches or traveler stone-piles - You cannot be harmed while inside. If the structure is toppled or broken, you are ejected and stunned for 4 rounds.
  • Water - Water Habitations are any body of water large enough for you to be submerged in, but cannot be too large (Example: The Ocean, sea, or large river would be too big, but you could Inhabit a cove, a tributary, a creek, a pond, or a section of a lake.) - While inside, you can move with a speed of 200 ft. and can exert your will on the water to cause some effects while you are Inhabiting it. Any pollution or effects (such as electricity, poison, etc) that afflict the water also afflict you. If the water is reduced or displaced, you are ejected and stunned for 4 rounds. You can sustain one of the below effects as a full-round action while Inhabiting a water Habitation:
    • Flow Shift - You may alter the flow of a current by 1 stage +1 stage per 5 levels. Stages: Reduce or increase flow speed from strong to moderate, moderate to weak, weak to still. One stage can also be used to rotate flow direction 45 degrees.
    • Mystic Water - You may restore the vitality of creatures who bathe in the water (Yourself included) by granting Fast Healing 1. When a creature has recovered a number of HP equal to twice their level, they no longer benefit. In addition, poisons do not progress or harm them while bathing.
    • Troubled Waters - You may increase the DC of any creatures to swim through the water by twice your level. Creatures with a swim speed treat it as difficult terrain.
  • Tree - Tree Habitations are any tree that has sprouted - You cannot be seen by basic magical detection when inside. If the tree is damaged while you are inside, you experience any pain and take any damage or effects the tree suffers. You may exit a Tree Habitation as 1 action and move up to your speed invisibly. If your Tree Habitation is destroyed while you are inside, you are reduced to -1 HP and are ejected in a dying state.


Attune: Whenever you Inhabit, your Sprite Attunement shifts to match your Habitation. Your Attunement remains the same until you enter a new Habitation. You gain several bonuses depending on your current Sprite Attunement:

  • Stone - Gain a +2 bonus to Constitution and a +2 racial bonus to CMD. In addition, you have DR 2/-.
  • Water - Gain a +2 bonus to Strength, the ability to breathe underwater, and a 40 foot swim speed.
  • Tree - Gain a +2 bonus to Dexterity, a +2 racial bonus to Acrobatics, Stealth, and a +2 natural armor bonus to AC.
