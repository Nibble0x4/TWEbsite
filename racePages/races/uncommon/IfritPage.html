The Ifrit Race


History
The Ifrit have a muddled history of assorted pride and shame. For many, their appearance is a sign of Vulcan's blessing and favor. As a result, it is not uncommon for citizens of Enthan to see the arrival of an Ifrit to be a sign of the merit of its parents. The parents, on the other hand, face the difficulty of raising a spirit of flame who is filled with the raging passion of their element and carries no regard whatsoever for social rules. The Wastes of Enthan have, in recent generations, become an excellent place for Ifrit who experience trouble fitting in at home. As a result, stories and legends abound of the great and daring exploits of the Ifrit tribes, scouring the desert sands in search of worthy prey to prove themselves against.

Appearance
Ifrits vary in appearance as widely as their elemental ancestors do. Most have pointy ears, red or mottled horns on the brow, and hair that flickers and waves as if it were aflame. Some possess skin the color of polished brass or have charcoal-hued scales covering their arms and legs. Ifrits favor revealing and ostentatious clothing in bright oranges and reds, preferably paired with gaudy jewelry.

Culture
Ifrits are most often born into human communities. Ifrits are frightening and volatile, so those who grow up in a city are almost always imprisoned or driven off before they reach adulthood; most are simply too hot-headed and independent to fit into civilized society, and their predilection toward pyromania doesn’t endear them to the local authorities. Those born into nomadic or tribal societies fare much better, since ifrits’ instinctive urge to explore and conquer their surroundings can easily earn them a place among their tribe’s leadership. Most Ifrits are born in Enthan under the blessing of Vulcan. These Ifrit are often considered blessed or sacred, but that does little to assuage the fearful effect their appearance and actions have on most civilized folk. The Wastes, however, are incredibly appealing to them. There is little there that can restrict or control them and the unbearable daytime heat is invigorating to the Ifrit. Ifrit often find their ways to the Wastes and spend their lives traveling across the sands in search of excitement and a community who shares their passion.

Functionality
Ifrit do not prefer to wait around. They enjoy acting, and they enjoy doing so in as flashy and bold a way as they can imagine. They rarely have the patience required for traditional divine or primal magics, but they are often seen dashing into battle or summoning hellfire to envelop their foes with a satisifed grin upon their faces.

Racial Traits
These are the basic traits natural to Ifrits

Ability Scores
Ifrits are passionate and quick, but impetuous and destructive.

+2 Dex, +2 Cha, -2 Wis

Base Speed
Ifrit have a base speed of 30 feet.

Size
Ifrits are Medium creatures and thus have no bonuses or penalties due to their size.

Type
Ifrits are outsiders with the native subtype.

Defense Racial Traits

Energy resistance: Ifrits halve all fire damage they take. In addition, they have fire resistance equal to their level.
Energy Weakness: Ifrits are weak their elemental opposite, cold. They gain Weakness to cold damage, taking double damage from it. Any effect that would give them resistance to cold is halved. Any effect that would make them immune to cold instead grants cold resistance 10 (After the half)

Senses

Dark Vision: Ifrits can see perfectly in the dark up to 60 feet

Elemental Abilities
The Ifrit are closely affiliated with fire, being descended from fire elementals. As a result, they have the innate power to manipulate fire by expending energy from within themselves. Ifrit have a Fire Pool, a pool of points that regenerate after a full night's sleep and contain a maximum number of points equal to 3 + 1/2 their level. They can expend these points to cast from a list of spells at will. Each spell has a different cost associated with it, shown in parentheses.
