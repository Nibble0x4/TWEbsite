The Merfolk Race


History
The Merfolk have a rich and glorious history, full of mighty conflict and proud contests. They have always thought themselves to be the chosen of the sea, the strongest and most beautiful creatures to grace its depths. They are correct on several accounts. They are powerful, strong and swift, and undeniably alluring. They have long seen the sea as their birthright and have sought true foes to contest them for it. Sea monsters fall before their mighty hunting parties and other humanoid races simply cannot contend with their swift swimming and their strong attacks. It is only the rare exception who can truly compete with them. The Endura managed it once, legend says, but they have long since retreated to their Depths without the aid of their Duty Bond partners. Now, the Merfolk war with each other, forming kingdoms that fight each other for glory and honor. They defend the sea jealously from those who would take it from them, both for their own amusement and for the protection of the wildlife and the natural order.

Appearance
Merfolk have the upper torsos of well-built and attractive humans and lower halves consisting of the tail and fins of a great fish. Their hair and scales span a wide range of hues, with Merfolk in a given region closely resembling each other. Merfolk can breathe air freely but move on dry land only with difficulty, and rarely spend long periods out of water. As a race, Merfolk are insular and distrustful of strangers, but individuals, especially adventuring Merfolk, break the mold and can be quite garrulous. Merfolk concern themselves more with nature and the arts than with morality and ethical debates, and have a strong inclination toward neutral alignments.

Culture
The Merfolk are a powerful and warlike race, revelling in the superiority they enjoy over all other races of the sea. They relish combat and the glory that can be won therein, as well as the satisfaction of seducing surfacers to their dooms in the briny depths. They are not evil, exactly. They would certainly describe themselves as majestic, but the rest of the sea is wary of their aggression. To those who befriend them, however, they are stalwart and upbeat allies who never back down from any challenge. The values of gallantry and honor are alive and well in Merfolk culture, encouraging self-sacrifice and courage. To those who can get past the dangerous exterior, Merfolk culture is filled with art and competition, the results of a people dedicated to self-improvement and greatness.

Functionality
The Merfolk are a powerful and proud race, specializing in roles that require daring action to achieve glorious victory. Front-line warriors are a favorite, but it isn't unheard of for one to be seen casting powerful spells from afar.

Racial Traits
These are the basic traits natural to Merfolk

Ability Scores
Merfolk are graceful, hale, and beautiful. They gain different ability scores depending on their gender

Male: +2 str, +2 con, +2 cha, -2 wis

Female: +2 dex, +2 con, +2 cha, -2 wis

Base Speed
Merfolk have different movement speeds in water and on land.

Land: 5 feet.

Swim: 50 feet.

Size
Merfolk are Medium creatures and thus have no bonuses or penalties due to their size.

Type
Merfolk are humanoids with the aquatic subtype.

Defense Racial Traits

Scale Armor: Merfolk have a +1 natural armor bonus.
Legless: Merfolk have no legs, and therefore cannot be tripped.

Senses

Low-Light Vision: Merfolk have low-light vision allowing them to see twice as far as humans in conditions of dim light.

Musical Abilities
These are the magical abilities gained by all Merfolk, channeled through their musical affinity.

Siren Song: The Merfolk are naturally charismatic and alluring, with a long history of luring others to their graves. They can cast the Charm hex as a witch of 1st level, using their charisma in place of their intelligence. When they do this, the hex is considered to be an enchantment spell.

Enchanting: The Merfolk have learned to leverage their beauty through magic, gaining a +2 bonus on DCs for any enchantment spells they cast.

Sea Singer: The Merfolk are musically inclined by nature. They gain a +2 bonus to perform(Sing) checks OR a +1 bonus to any perform skill they choose.

Song of the Sea: When the Merfolk are deep in the sea, they can hear the whispers of the ocean. The song inspires them to greater feats, granting them the ability to reroll any single d20 roll they make once a day, as long as the original roll wasn't a natural 1.

Other Racal Traits

Amphibious: Merfolk are amphibious, but prefer not to spend long periods out of the water.
