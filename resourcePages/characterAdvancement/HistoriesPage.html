Histories


Each character in TWE has a past. What were they like before they started traveling the world as an adventurer?

Histories


Acrobat


You worked as an acrobat, likely for a circus or similar group.


Connection to the World: You have a connection with your employer and another performer.
Situational Advantage: You can, at will, before rolling an acrobatics check, declare the maneuver to be a 'daring' one. If so, the DC increases by 10 but success and failure become more dramatic.
Skill Bonus: You gain a +2 bonus on acrobatics checks.
My Costume: Begin play with a masterwork set of Performer's Apparel that also grants a +2 bonus on acrobatics checks.


Assassin


You work as an assassin, a paid hand instructed to kill others without question.


Connection to the World: Select a Mark. You have been tasked with killing this person and you may know your employer.
Situational Advantage: When you hit your Mark unaware, you score a crit and gain a bonus to damage on all attacks. When you kill your Mark, you may select a new one.
Skill Bonus: You gain a +2 bonus on any disguise, bluff, stealth, or sleight of hand checks made to hunt your target.
My Blade: Begin play with a higher-quality weapon of your choice. This weapon is enchanted against your target.


Beast Tamer


You have spent a lot of time around a specific kind of animal, learning the subtleties of their mannerisms and how to calm them.


Connection to the World: Select a location and a type of beast that exists there. Monsters are not beasts. You know one fellow enthusiast or perhaps a poacher.
Situational Advantage: You have intimate knowledge of this creature and their place in the food chain.
Skill Bonus: You have a +2 bonus on handle animal checks. This bonus triples against your chosen type of beast.
My Little Friend: Begin play with a young or adolescent version of your chosen beast type. This little friend is not combat trained but can perform some tricks.


Con Artist


You made a living for yourself by tricking others into giving you money willingly.


Connection to the World: You have spent the greater part of your life tricking people. There is one ploy or act that you are skilled at performing. You can take 10 on checks related to this act. You may have had a partner in crime.
Situational Advantage: You may create an alter ego, a separate person you pretend to be while tricking others. Your crimes are linked to this alter ego.
Skill Bonus: You know the tells of the business, and gain a +2 bonus on Bluff and Sense Motive checks.
My Alter Ego: You begin play with a masterwork set of clothing and supplies needed to perform your act. You have +4 bonus on bluff and disguise checks to pass as this person.


Craftsman


You worked with your hands, creating something that interests you...or at least payed the bills.


Connection to the World: Select a type of craft. You are a skilled and somewhat reputable craftsman of that trade. You have a connection with your master or your apprentice. You have a home in the form of a permanent workshop that you can develop.
Situational Advantage: You have a home in the form of a permanent workshop that you can develop.
Skill Bonus: You gain a +2 bonus on craft, knowledge, and profession checks that apply to your trade.
My Finest Work: Begin play with a master-crafted item of a suitable type to your trade.


Cultist


You were in a cult at some point, one that shaped your understanding of the world and ignited an interest in the darker and more mysterious sides of the world.


Connection to the World: Select a region or a cult. You were inducted into this cult and its teachings still haunt you. You know a mentor who inducted you and someone you inducted.
Situational Advantage: You know the tenants of a cult and retain some of its values. You are aware of some of their locations or rituals.
Skill Bonus: You gain a +2 bonus on Knowledge checks about cults. You gain a +4 bonus on bluff and disguise checks made to pass as a member of a cult you know much about.
My Talisman: If you choose to be true to the teachings of your cult, you begin play with a talisman that contains some of the power of the cult.


Devout


You revere a specific deity, admiring them for their power, values, or actions. You need not be a religious class to take this history.


Connection to the World: Select a deity. You spent a lot of time at this deity's shrine and learned their ways, dedicating yourself to their teachings. You know 1 fellow acolyte and 1 mentor.
Situational Advantage: You have already sworn yourself to this deity's creed and gained a quest from them. If you complete it, you will become their servant.
Skill Bonus: You gain a bonus of +2 on Knowledge Religion checks. This bonus is tripled if it concerns your deity.
My Reward: You have received a magical token as a result of your devotion. Select a trinket and discuss with your GM the power it holds. It gets stronger with your devotion. You have less money for other items.


Drunkard


You have spent a significant portion of your life black-out drunk, carrying something of a reputation for it.


Connection to the World: Select a tavern. You have a negative reputation there but the owner accommodates you.
Situational Advantage: While drunk, lethal damage is converted into nonlethal damage.
Skill Bonus: You have a dynamic relationship with alcohol. You can gain a +4 bonus or a -4 penalty on Fortitude saves against drunkenness at will.
My Flask: Begin play with a worn flask. Any liquid you drink from it with a negative effect has a chance to negate that effect. You have less money for other stuff.


Entertainer


You worked as an entertainer, either as a solo act or a performing group. Choose whether you were primarily traveling or local.


Connection to the World: You know someone who works at an establishment you often frequented and likely some fellow entertainers.
Situational Advantage: If Traveling, then select up to 3 towns where you commonly stopped. There is a tavern in each where you are welcome to stay for free and will more easily gather a group when performing.
If Local, then select one town where you worked. The local tavern there is a second home to you, allowing you to stay for free, more easily gather crowds for performances, and you have one friend there who owes you several favors.
Skill Bonus: You gain a +2 bonus on Perform checks of a specified type. This bonus is doubled if in a town you selected as a traveling act or tripled for a solo act.
My Biggest Tip: You may select a single higher-quality set of clothing, light armor piece, or magical trinket.


Guard


You worked as a guard, protecting a settlement of some kind.


Connection to the World: Select a town where you were stationed. You will have connection with 1 superior officer and 1 fellow or lower officer.
Situational Advantage: You know about the borders of the town and know enough about the hierarchy to request a meeting.
Skill Bonus: You gain a +2 bonus on perception checks and sense motive checks rolled when standing guard over an area.
My Old Friend: You begin play with 1 weapon of your choice, suitable to the place they worked, and it is Masterwork


Healer


You worked as a healer, magical or otherwise, and are closely acquainted with the pain of the afflicted.


Connection to the World: Select a location. You worked as a healer there for some time and you know both a mentor and someone you greatly helped.
Situational Advantage: You are adept at assessing the ailing. You can get a more accurate reading on a creature's robustness and remaining life.
Skill Bonus: You gain a +2 bonus on Heal checks.
My First Aid Kit: You begin play with an enchanted tool or tool set that you can use to restore HP over time once a day.


Hunter


You lived off the land, hunting the beasts to ensure you had enough to survive.


Connection to the World: Select a place where you hunted game and a prized kill.
Situational Advantage: You are intimately familiar with the habits of creatures similar to your prized kill. You are excellent at determining what kinds of animals are in an area.
Skill Bonus: You gain a +2 bonus on survival checks to track. This bonus doubles against your chosen type.
My Prized Kill: You begin play with an item made from the creature you are most proud of killing.


Lumberjack


You worked as a lumberjack, chopping wood in relative loneliness.


Connection to the World: Select a forest. You know a merchant you sell your lumber to when you worked there.
Situational Advantage: You have proficiency with handaxes. If you are proficient with them from your class, you may gain the Weapon Focus feat for it.
Skill Bonus: You have a +2 bonus on survival checks and climb checks in similar environments to where you worked.
My Trusty Hatchet: You begin play with a masterwork handaxe weapon.


Merchant


You have spent your life working to make ends meet through buying and selling.


Connection to the World: Select a town. You have a couple regulars who you have built relationships and trust with.
Situational Advantage: You begin play with a permanent shop building that is customizable somewhere you used to work.
Skill Bonus: You gain a +2 bonus on Appraise and Diplomacy checks made during mercantile situations.
My Wares: Select a type of wares. You gain a stock of that kind of item kept in your shop.


Miner


You worked as a miner, building patience and muscle as you unearthed the treasures below.


Connection to the World: Select a mountain range. You know a mine manager here and at least 1 fellow miner.
Situational Advantage: You have proficiency with pickaxe weapons. If you are proficient with them from your class, you may choose the Weapon Focus feat for one.
Skill Bonus: You gain a +2 bonus on appraise checks regarding ore.
My Trusty Pick: You begin play with a masterwork pick type weapon.


Mystic Tribesman


You were raised in a tribe with occult rituals and traditions.


Connection to the World: Select a region. You are a member of a small tribe there where you each know each other. Your tribe is in touch with the other powers in TWE.
Situational Advantage: You are familiar with 1 tribal ritual and, with enough assistance from allies, can perform it using aetherless magic.
Disadvantage: You are tainted to the eyes of some magics, due to your dalliance with other powers. You cannot cast divine spells.
My Totem: You may select a single trinket to be imbued with aetherless magic or to serve as a connection to an other power. You have less money for other stuff.


Noble


You were born into wealth and privilege, living a life others envy.


Connection to the World: Select a location. You are a part of a noble house in that region. You know your immediate family well and have a close relationship with a servant who helped raise you.
Situational Disadvantage: You are easier to identify than others and you are more likely to be targeted or identified as a rich target. You suffer a -2 on disguise checks to hide your identity.
Skill Bonus: You gain a +2 bonus on Knowledge(Nobility) checks.
My Insignia: You have a coat of arms that identifies you as part of your house. You Begin play with a single higher-quality weapon, armor, set of clothes, or magical trinket that bears your crest. You have more money for other items.


Peasant


You never meant much to the larger world. You were a farmer, a handyman, or just someone who worked an odd job wherever you could. Your friends and family love you and that has always been enough.


Connection to the World: Select a place where you grew up and worked. You have a circle of well-meaning but poorly equipped friends who will watch your back and help you when you are there.
Situational Advantage: You have a reputation among the normal folk in the region you lived. They will believe you and rally to you when needed.
Skill Bonus: You gain a +4 bonus on Knowledge Local and Knowledge Nobility checks in your town.
My Family's Pride: Begin play with a single higher-quality weapon or magical trinket. You have less money for other items.


Sailor


You spent much of your life on a sailing vessel of some kind, whether as a simple fisherman, a daring pirate or anything in between.


Connection to the World: Select a Ship and Crew. You are familiar with the people who manned this vessel.
Situational Advantage: In port, you are more likely to be able to haggle deals, identify ships and crews, and find your way around.
Skill Bonus: You are skilled on board a ship, possessing knowledge of the basics of the craft. You gain a +2 bonus on Profession(Sailor).
My Lucky Token: You may select a single small token to be imbued with a positive lucky boon.


Scholar


You were a student, working tirelessly to further your knowledge of some subject.


Connection to the World: Select a place where you were learning and a subject. You know a professor and a classmate from that class.
Situational Advantage: You have a photographic memory, or close to it. You can use this to ask questions to the GM about places you have been or situations you experienced without rolling a wisdom check.
Skill Bonus: You gain a +4 bonus on Knowledge checks regarding your subject.
My Notes: You have a notebook filled with obscure details. You can use it to roll a percentage and perhaps find information that slipped your mind. You start play with less money for other things.


Servant


You worked as a servant, choosing to fill a service role in exchange for room, board, and fair wages.


Connection to the World: Select a House or place in TWE. You worked as a servant there and know the masters of the house and at least 1 other servant.
Situational Advantage: You have served your house well and have a friendly home to return to if you need.
Skill Bonus: You are skilled at operating in plain sight, unnoticed. Gain a +2 bonus on stealth, disguise, and bluff checks to blend into the background when doing a mundane activity.
My Letter of Recommendation: Begin play with one higher quality magical trinket that bears the symbol of the house you served.


Soldier


You worked as a soldier, fighting the wars you were called on to fight.


Connection to the World: Select a province. You served as a member of the armed forces for that province. You have a relationship with 1 comrade-in-arms and 1 superior officer.
Situational Advantage: You have the proof of your service, enough to get some level of respect from officials and guards.
Skill Bonus: You have experience in the grueling life of a soldier, and have learned to sleep in your armor without penalty.
My Nation's Weapon: You begin play with a particularly high quality masterwork weapon matching that used by your military.


Storyteller


You worked as a soldier, fighting the wars you were called on to fight.


Connection to the World: You know a small collection of stories that are very important to you, told to you by a mentor you care deeply for. You may choose historical events to have stories of or leave it to the GM.
Situational Advantage: You are skilled at identifying the nuances of storytelling. When you are presented with a narrative or historical tale, you may choose to trust your instincts. If you do, roll a d100 and the GM may tell you whether the story is true, based in truth, conjecture, legend, modified history, or unfounded.
Skill Bonus: Gain a +2 bonus on Perform(Orate). You may use Perform(Orate) in place of Diplomacy or Bluff when you spin a tale to assist you.
My Soap Box: You begin play with some sort of item that you use to make yourself more easily seen or heard (such as a box you stand on). While using this item and remaining still, you can double the effective range of your oration and gain a +2 bonus to your Perform(Orate) skill.


Survivalist


You have lived a long time surviving off the land in a region.


Connection to the World: Select a biome. You know one hermit who also lived out there.
Situational Advantage: You are adept at gathering and can identify helpful or harmful flora you find.
Skill Bonus: You gain a +2 bonus on survival checks and this bonus is doubled in your biome.
My Lodestone: Begin play with a mundane trinket of your choice. You always know which direction leads to this trinket. You have less money for other stuff.


Thief


You made ends meet any way you could... primarily by stealing from others.


Connection to the World: You have committed a great theft at one point in your life. You know of one mark that you hit somewhere in TWE, likely a noble house or equivalent.
Situational Advantage: You have thorough knowledge of that mark and, if needed, you could likely find a way inside again.
Skill Bonus: You are experienced in taking things that aren't yours. You gain a +2 bonus on Sleight of Hand checks.
My Greatest Take: You begin play with 2 higher quality sets of clothing, weapons, or magical trinkets stolen from the mark. They are identifiable as belonging to the mark by anyone who might care.


Thug


You are no stranger to getting what you want from others by force.


Connection to the World: Select a town. You terrorized the streets there and may have been incarcerated. You have connection to 1 partner in crime or someone you met as an inmate.
Situational Advantage: You have the power to force a creature who is fleeing due to fear to stop, feeling like they can't escape.
Skill Bonus: You are confident in the art of bluster. You gain a +2 bonus on intimidate checks and bluff checks made bluster.
My Lunch Money: You begin play with an assortment of cheap, low-quality items that can easily be sold.











Detrimental Histories


Not every adventurer had a pleasant past. Some are haunted by past mistakes. These Histories offer interesting roleplay opportunities but do inflict difficulties upon characters who take them.


Cursed


You were subject to a powerful curse. This curse is highly difficult to remove, resistant to the regular methods.


Connection to the World: Pick a place and a curse source. You were cursed in some way, and it is sticking around.
Situational Disadvantage: You have a detrimental curse and potentially a way to reverse it. It is powerful and cannot be removed by Remove Curse.
Skill Bonus: You have a +4 bonus on will saves against curse effects and have some innate knowledge of curses. You can cast spend a few minutes with an item to determine if it is cursed and to what degree.
My Cursed Relic: You MAY begin play with some sort of cursed item in your possession.


Debted


You are in a lot of debt, whether due to foolishness or poor luck. You are going to have to work hard to get rid of it.


Connection to the World: Pick a person in a place. You know this person and may know another who also owes them money.
Situational Disadvantage: You owe this person a debt, whether monetary or otherwise, and will be hunted or restricted until it has been accomplished.
Skill Bonus: You gain a +2 bonus on Appraise and Bluff checks regarding money and an item's value.
My Brand: Your debtor has branded you as a symbol of your debt. You can choose to have the same amount of starting money or less. If same, then you are currently being hunted.


Runner


You were raised in Port Bismuth under the Lady, made to run for her service.


Connection to the World: You have a designation number. You know the names and numbers of several fellow Runners.
Situational Advantage: You are used to pushing your body. You gain the Endurance feat and almost always win a chase.
Skill Bonus: You are excellent at running away from your problems. You gain a +2 bonus on Escape artist checks.
My Piece of Her: You stole something from The Lady's mansion when she was killed. Select a much higher quality set of clothing, jewelry, or magical trinket. You have less money for other items.


Slave


You were taken as a slave and the shadow of this has been over you for much of your life.


Connection to the World: Select a place and person. This is your master. You know your master and at least 1 other slave. You are easily recognized by them.
Situational Advantage: You have a much easier time pretending to be a slave or servant.
Skill Bonus: You gain a +2 bonus on will saves against mind-affecting effects due to your inner defiance.
My Brand: You bear a slave's brand, the mark of your captors or owner. You have a higher-quality trinket from your master. You have less money for other stuff.
