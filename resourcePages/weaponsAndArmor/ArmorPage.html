Armor and Shields
That Which Exists (TWE) is a dangerous place. Regardless of who you are, you are likely going to find yourself in some sort of a fight if you are traveling across TWE. Some travelers are content with their ordinary clothes, relying on their skills, speed, or magic to protect them. Others, however, wish to better prepare themselves to survive when deadly foes confront them. These people are likely interested in some form of armor.

Depending on an adventurer's class, they will be more or less proficient in the wearing of armor. It is very important to consider your armor proficiency before donning it. When you are wearing armor you are not proficient with, you suffer the following effects:
- Your dexterity modifier is reduced by 2 if the armor is light, 4 if the armor is medium, and 6 if the armor is heavy. If this penalty reduces your dexterity bonus to 0, then you are unable to move in the armor.
- You suffer a penalty on attack rolls and damage rolls with physical weapons equal to the penalty mentioned above.
- You suffer a penalty on CMB checks, CMD, Reflex Saves, and concentration checks equal to the penalty mentioned above.
- You suffer a penalty on dex-or-str-based skill checks equal to the penalty mentioned above.
- You cannot Charge or Run.
- You suffer -1 action on your turn in combat.



If you are unsure about Hardness and weapon or armor HP, check out the Materials Page!



Each armor has a set of statistics in the following categories, represented by columns in the table below:
Slot: The part of your body that the armor is intended to cover. There are three regions: The Head, the Torso, and the Legs. Bonuses and penalties of the armors worn in different slots do stack together.
Name: The name of the armor itself.
AC Bonus: The bonus to AC granted by wearing the armor.
DR/X: Damage reduction applied to physical attacks received while wearing the armor. X denotes the type of physical damage that bypasses this DR.
Dex AC Penalty: When a wearer has a bonus to their AC as a result of their Dex, this number is a penalty to that bonus. If the combined penalties are greater than the wearer's Dex bonus to AC, then they simply do not add their Dex to their AC. This penalty cannot apply a penalty to a wearer's AC by overcoming their Dex.
Movement Penalty: This number is subtracted from a wearer's base movement speed when they don the armor.
Arcane Interference: When the wearer casts a spell with Somatic components, they must roll 1d100. If their result is less than or equal to this chance, then they roll on the Aetheric Interference Table to determine what happens.
Requirements: These are additional specific requirements a wearer must meet in order to be considered proficient with this armor.
Special: This contains a tag describing any special traits specific to the armor.

Tag Information



Special Tag Descriptions
Each of the tags in the 'Special' columns above are explained here.

The abilities represented by the Special tags are available to any wielder who is proficient with that weapon.


Covering - This shield grants the wielder and anyone behind them full cover from ranged attacks.


Crumpling - When the wearer suffers a critical hit, the armor restricts the ability of the skin to heal the damage from that wound.


Fragile - The armor has half the usual HP for an armor of its material.


Fluid - The AC bonus granted by this armor is included in your Touch AC.


Grounded - The DR of this armor also counts as Elemental Resistance to Electricity.


Impact - This armor adds 1d6 damage to successful Bullrush attempts and attacks that involve falling onto the target.


Insulating - The DR of this armor also counts as Elemental Resistance to Cold.


Lightning Rod - This armor or shield attracts electricity. Any attacks with electricity in them gain a +3 bonus on attack rolls against the wearer. In addition, any lightning effects that end within 5 feet of the wearer are considered to include them in the range.


Moving - The armorâ€™s AC bonus is only granted on attacks of opportunity provoked by movement or while dancing.


Piecemeal - Whenever the wearer of this armor is damaged by a melee attack, a piece of the armor falls off and the AC bonus of this armor is reduced by 1.


Polished - Once per turn, when a melee attack misses you, inflict Dazzled EB for 1 turn. This effect is nullified if the armor is dirtied.


Reflect - Once per turn, when a ray attack misses you, you may choose any direction to reflect it in. The attack roll of the attack is applied to the AC of the first creature in that direction. A natural 1 attack roll is treated as a natural 20 for the purposes of this ability.


Ricochet - Once per turn, when a ranged projectile attack misses you, you may choose a direction to deflect it in. It cannot be the same direction the attack came from. The attack roll of the attacker is applied to the AC of the first creature in that direction. A natural 1 attack roll is treated as a natural 20 for the purposes of this ability.


Sturdy - This armor grants a 2 + EB bonus on CMD against against Trip, Bullrush, Reposition and Drag maneuvers.


Survivalist - This armor grants resistance to damage from moving through environments such as harsh winds, thorny briars, and harsh crags.


Thick - The DR of this armor also counts as Elemental Resistance to Acid.


Weak Fire - The armor is damaged directly by any attack that deals fire damage. This damage bypasses hardness.
