Materials
That Which Exists is full of powerful weapons, stout armors, and curious items. What happens, however, when these things are damaged?

Like characters, items have HP and AC. These function similarly to character HP and AC, with the following differences:

  1. Items cannot be healed by healing spells or hurt by most effects the way people are. Most attacks, unless they are Sunder maneuver or specifically noted to do damage to items, do not harm weapons, armor, or items.
  2. Items can be repaired by a craftsman or some spells designed to heal items.
  3. An unattended item has an AC of 10. An attended item's AC is ignored and replaced with the CMD of the one attending it. It must then be targeted using a Sunder maneuver.
  4. Items also possess a Hardness value. This hardness functions as DR/- that is subtracted from all incoming damage before it applies to the HP of the item.
  5. When items are reduced to 0 HP, they gain the Broken condition. If they are reduced to an amount of negative HP equal to their max HP, they are completely destroyed.



- The Broken condition renders the item useless, removing any armor bonuses from armor, turning any weapon into an improvised weapon, and negating any helpful special abilities tied to them. Each item differs when it is broken, but it is never good.



On this page you will find a collection of materials that exist in TWE and can be used to make weapons, armor, and items.
The materials used for an item change the Hardness, HP, and AC of the item in question. They can also sometimes grant strange effects to any items made of them.

HERE you will find a helpful reference table for the standard HP of regular armor, weapons, and shields.

Materials


Mithril


This pale-blue metal gleams like silver when it catches the light. It weighs far less than it looks like it should.


Armor: Mithril armor is lighter than others of its kind. The Str requirement to wear it is reduced by 2 and the dexterity penalty is reduced by 1.
Weapons: Mithril weapons are lightweight and easy to wield, reducing the penalty for fighting with two weapons by 2. This applies to Mithril Double weapons as well. If the weapon has a Str or Con requirement, reduce it by 2.
Hardness: Mithril items have Hardness 12.


Adamantine


This radiant near-white steel has been refined to perfection, granting it nigh unmatched durability. It is very difficult to fashion.


Armor: Admantine armor is tougher than others of its kind, doubling the DR value of the armor. It is heavy, though, increasing the Str requirement to wear the armor by 2.
Weapons: Adamantine weapons double their Masterwork and Enhancement bonuses to Attack and Damage.
Hardness: Adamantine items have Hardness 20.


Arcanite


This deep blue crystal sparks with wild magic power. It stores arcane magic and reacts violently, sometimes uncontrollably, to new magic sources. It is rare and very expensive.


Armor: Arcanite can be used in armors that are made of metal or stone. It is less hardy than metal, reducing the AC bonus of the armor by 1 and cutting the DR in half. Arcanite Armors grant SR equal to their AC bonus and Elemental Resistance equal to their DR. Enchantments are more intense in Arcanite Armor.
Weapons: Arcanite can be used in weapons made of metal or stone. Arcanite weapons cannot add their Enhancement Bonuses to their attack or damage. Enchantments are more intense in Arcanite Weapons.
Hardness: Arcanite items have Hardness 6. When broken, an Arcanite item releases a surge of unpredictable magic. An Arcane caster wielding or wearing a broken Arcanite item can roll a Spellcraft check to harness the surging energy and try to control the result.


Bone


This deathly material gives an archaic look to its creations. It has some affiliation with Death.


Armor: Bone armor gains the Fragile tag. Add the DR bonus of Bone Armor to saves against death effects, necromancy spells, and negative energy effects. The DR of Bone Armor applies against negative energy damage.
Weapons: Bone Weapons gain the Fragile tag and the Critical tag.
Hardness: Bone items have Hardness 8.


Blood Crystal


This crimson crystal seems to pulse as light falls on it. It attracts and absorbs blood into itself, gaining a deeper red color for a time.


Armor: Blood Crystal Armor absorbs your blood and infuses it back into you, negating bleed damage each round equal to its DR.
Weapons: Blood Crystal Weapons draw the blood out of your foes, inflicting 1 point of stacking bleed damage per successful strike. All attacks with a Blood Crystal weapon deal +1 damage against a bleeding target.
Hardness: Blood Crystal items have Hardness 8. When broken, a Blood Crystal item pours forth all the blood it consumed.


Frigite


This freezing ore is found deep in Gruss, mined by only the hardiest, and is very difficult to refine. It has a strong affiliation for the cold.


Armor: Frigite Armor gains the Weak Fire tag. Frigite Armor deals cold damage to its wearer each turn equal to its DR. The Armor provides twice its DR as ER against Cold.
Weapons: Frigite Weapons gain the Weak Fire tag. Light weapons deal 2 points of cold damage per turn to their wielder. 1-Handed weapons and ranged weapons deal 4, and 2-handed weapons deal 8. They add this damage to attacks made with the weapon.
Hardness: Frigite items have Hardness 8. When broken, a burst of cold air surges forth.


Cold Steel


This alloy of Frigite and Steel is full of cold energy and is the most effective at subduing fey beings.


Armor: Cold Steel armor provides its DR as ER against Cold. Fae creatures wearing Cold Steel armor take double its DR in damage every turn and have all of their fae powers neutralized. Wearers of this armor gain a bonus on saves against fae effects equal to the highest DR piece of Cold Steel armor they are wearing.
Weapons: Cold Steel weapons deal additional damage equal to their Enhancement Bonus as cold damage. These weapons deal double damage to fae creatures and, on a critical hit, neutralize their powers.
Hardness: Cold Steel items have Hardness 10.


Vitalwood


This wood refuses to die, sprouting new growths in the form of sprouts, twigs, leaves, and flowers from barren logs laying in the sunlight.


Armor: Vitalwood Armor is quick to mend, healing itself for 1 HP every hour that it spends in the sunlight. Fearthites can damage their armor as an action in order to heal themselves for the amount damaged.
Weapons: Vitalwood Weapons are quick to mend, healing themselves for 1 HP every hour that they spend in the sunlight. Fearthites can damage their weapon as an action in order to heal themselves for the amount damaged.
Hardness: Vitalwood items have Hardness 6.
