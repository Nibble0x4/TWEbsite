Weapons
That Which Exists (TWE) is a dangerous place. Regardless of who you are, you are likely going to find yourself in some sort of a fight if you are traveling across TWE. When it comes down to it, everyone needs a weapon. Sure, some people are better at using them than others, but everyone needs a weapon at some point in their adventures.

Tag Information



Special Tag Descriptions
Each of the tags in the 'Special' columns above are explained here.

The abilities represented by the Special tags are available to any wielder who is proficient with that weapon.


Bardic (X)- You may use this weapon as an instrument of X type if you are a bard.


Brace- If you have a readied action prepared and you are attacked by a charging creature, you may make an attack roll against them with a +2 bonus on the attack roll and deal double damage with that attack.


Breathing- In order to use this weapon, you must be able to breathe.


Bullrush- You may use this weapon during a bullrush to gain a +2 bonus on the check.


Composite- You add your strength modifier to damage with this weapon in addition to your dex modifier.


Composite+- You add your strength modifier to damage with this weapon in addition to your dex modifier. For every 2 your strength score is higher than the strength requirement for this weapon, increase the base damage dice of this weapon by 1.


Con X- You must have a constitution score of at least X in order to wield this weapon without becoming fatigued.


Critical- During a critical hit or a soft crit, you may reroll each of your damage dice once, if you wish, and take the new result.


Crit-Guarded- While wielding this weapon, your crit guard is increased by 2.


Dex X- You must have a dexterity score of at least X in order to wield this weapon without suffering a -6 penalty on attack rolls.


Disarm- When you use this weapon to try and disarm an opponent, you gain +2 bonus on the check.


Double- You may choose an end of the weapon to be considered the primary weapon and use the other as a secondary weapon. You may choose to spend one action to make an attack with each end of the weapon at a -2 penalty. You may only do this once on your turn and may not do it during an attack of opportunity.


Dramatic- Perform or intimidate checks made with this weapon gain a +2 bonus.


Feral- This weapon may be worn atop suitable natural weapon and adds its damage to the base damage of the natural weapon.


Finesse- This weapon can be used with the Weapon Finesse and Deadly Grace feats.


Fixed- This weapon takes at least a minute to equip but cannot be disarmed.


Guarded- When you use the Defend action, gain a +1 bonus to our AC.


Hateful- This weapon was crafted to defeat a specific type of creature and gains a +4 bonus on attack rolls and damage rolls against them.


Hidden- You gain a +3 bonus to Sleight of Hand checks to conceal this weapon or to disguise it as a nonthreatening object.


Loading X- Making ranged attacks with this weapon requires you to spend X actions reloading it first. An ally who can reach you can spend actions to assist you in this.


Melee- This ranged weapon can be used as a melee weapon with a -2 penalty on the attack roll.


Monk- Monks are proficient with this weapon and may use it to perform a Flurry of Blows.


Nonlethal- Attacks made with this weapon deal nonlethal damage.


Powered- This weapon requires an elemental stone or alchemical item as a power source in order to be used properly. The power source used has an impact on the weapon.


Prone- You can only operate this weapon from a prone position.


Reach- This weapon increases your threatening range by 5 feet.


Reach X- This weapon increases your threatening range by X feet.


Shield- If you attack with this shield, you take a -2 penalty on the attack and lose its shield bonus to AC.


Steal- When you land a critical hit with this weapon, you may attempt a Steal combat maneuver against your target as a free action.


Str X- You must have a strength score of at least X in order to wield this weapon without suffering a -4 penalty on attack rolls and damage rolls.


Sunder- When you use this weapon to sunder, you gain a +2 bonus on the check.


Third Hand- This weapon can be used by a limb that cannot normally be used to make attacks, such as a leg or prehensile tail, and can make attacks with a -4 penalty once per turn. You can only make an attack with a Third Hand weapon once per turn.


Tool- This weapon is hardy enough to be used in situations that may call for other tools such as crowbars.


Trip- When you use this weapon to try to trip a creature and fail, you can choose to drop this weapon to avoid being tripped.


Focus Tag Descriptions
Each of the tags in the 'Focus' columns above are explained here.

The abilities represented by the Focus tags are available to any wielder who is proficient with that weapon and also has the Weapon Focus feat for that weapon.

There are some rules about how Focus abilities are used:
1- Unless otherwise stated in the ability, you may only use each Focus ability once in a turn. (Meaning you may use multiple different Focus abilities in one turn as long as you don't use the same ability twice.)
2- Ability effects do not stack with themselves (meaning that if you Addle a foe and another creature also Addles that same foe, the effects do not stack. Instead, only the larger effect will remain.)
3- Unless otherwise stated, using a Focus ability requires you to spend a swift action after successfully landing an attack with the weapon.
4- To handle scaling, most Focus abilities refer to EB (the Enhancement Bonus of the weapon).
5- All effects applied to a creature as a result of your Focus abilities last only until the start of your next turn.
6- In order to use a Focus ability on a weapon, you must possess the Weapon Focus feat for that weapon type.


Addle- The target suffers a -(1 + EB) penalty on attack rolls until the start of your next turn.


Brawling- You may use the Defend action as a swift action.


Chop- If the attack is a critical hit, add 1 + EB d6s as bonus damage to the damage dealt (This damage is multiplied). If the attack is a soft crit, the damage is not multiplied.


Concuss- The target suffers a -(1 + EB) penalty on saving throws until the start of your next turn.


Crush- You may make a Sunder combat maneuver against your target as a swift action with a 1 + EB bonus on the check. If you are successful, you may deal half the damage of your attack to one of the target's pieces of worn or wielded equipment.


Dextrous- You may use your dexterity modifier in place of your strength modifier on attack rolls and damage rolls with this weapon. This does not require a successful attack roll or a swift action to use.


Disorient- The target may not use swift actions until the start of your next turn.


Distract- The target takes a -(2 + 2*EB) penalty on concentration checks until the start of your next turn.


Dueling- If your off-hand is free, you may gain a 2 + EB bonus on your next attack roll.


Gash- The target receives an 'Open Wound' where your attack struck. This wound has an AC equal to the target's Crit Guard - (2 + EB). Any attack that strikes this wound gains an additional 1 + EB d6 base damage. Aiming for and missing this wound results in a miss of the entire target.


Hook- You may attempt a Reposition combat maneuver as a swift action with a 1 + EB bonus on the check.


Knock- You may make a CMB check against the target with a 1 + EB bonus on the check as a swift action. If you are successful, the target may not use their offhand to make attacks or to wield a weapon or shield or use a held item until the start of your next turn.


Pierce- If the attack is a called shot, add 1 + EB extra damage dice to the base damage of the attack (The dice size of this bonus damage is decided by the GM based on the difficulty of the called shot)


Push- You may make a Bullrush combat maneuver against your target as a swift action with a 1 + EB bonus on the check.


Shred- The target's armor bonus or natural armor bonus to AC is reduced by (1 + EB) until the start of your next turn.


Skewer- The target can no longer take 5-foot steps until the start of your next turn.


Slash- When you miss an attack roll with this weapon, you may roll attack again against another creature within reach as a swift action.


Tether- You may make a Grapple combat maneuver against your target as a swift action with a 1 + EB bonus on the check. If you are successful, the target's movement is restricted to your attack range unless they: Spend an action to free themselves (this provokes an attack of opportunity); Escape with an Escape Artist check opposed to your CMD + 1 + EB; Disarm you; or use a weapon to sunder this weapon. You may not use this weapon or any hands holding it to make attacks while the target is thus restrained.

Unique Tag Descriptions
The Unique weapons often have more cplex and unique abilities. Thus, this section will cover them separately.

The abilities represented by these special tags are unique to each of these weapons, scattered throughout TWE. They apply whenever a wielder of the weapons meets the criteria and wields the weapon in combat.


Shapeshift- You may, as a free action, change the solid black head of this hammer to the shape of any other weapon or tool you desire. This alters the damage type of the weapon accordingly and may be used in tandem with combat maneuvers or other creative plays.


Ammoless- This weapon does not require ammo to fire. Instead, it generates its own in the form of arrow-shaped bolts of energy.


Zodiac-Snake- This weapon fires a beam of energy that deals physical damage to everyone in a line. When you attack with it, you roll attack against every creature in the line and deal damage to anyone you strike. The damage dice double with every target you include in the line, starting with 1 for the first target. This doubling takes effect regardless of whether the attack hits each creature. (For example, if you fire at a line with Clive closest to you, Anna behind him, and Geoffrey furthest from you, and Anna is the only one who dodged the attack, then you would deal 1 die of damage to Clive and 4 dice of damage to Geoffrey.)


Zodiac-Ox- This weapon is also a shield, granting you a bonus to AC equal to 2 + the weapon's EB. This spiked shield causes anyone you successfully bullrush to become caught upon the spikes. If they are caught in this way, they take damage equal to the damage of the weapon and are considered weightless until the end of your bullrush. You may bullrush as many creatures in this way as you can include in a charge, as long as you continue to succeed on the Bullrush attempts. Anyone caught upon your spikes takes the spike damage again whenever a new creature is caught upon the spikes and, if the bullrush ends against a wall, they take fall damage as if they had fallen the distance between where they were caught and the wall.


Zodiac-Dog- This weapon is a lightweight shortsword that seems mundane. When you hold it, your voice is amplified at will to reach over larger distances. As long as you are wielding it, the radius of any beneficial sound-based effects or auras are doubled. In addition, when you are flanking with an ally, you both gain the following benefits:
- Whenever one of you misses an attack, the enemy provokes an attack of opportunity from your flanking partners.
- You gain an additional bonus to attack rolls equal to this weapon's EB.
- You gain 1d6 extra damage.
